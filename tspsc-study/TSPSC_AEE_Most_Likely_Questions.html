1. RCC & Prestressed Concrete Design (20-25 Questions Expected)

🎯 Exam Tip: Highest weightage subject! Focus on IS 456:2000 provisions, limit state design, beam/slab/column design, and development length. 4-5 numerical problems expected.

1.1 Working Stress & Limit State Method

Q1. As per IS 456, partial safety factor for concrete in limit state of collapse is: Ans: 1.5 (γm = 1.5)
Q2. Partial safety factor for steel in limit state of collapse is: Ans: 1.15 (γs = 1.15)
Q3. Design strength of concrete = 0.67fck/1.5 = : Ans: 0.447fck
Q4. Maximum strain in concrete at failure in limit state method is: Ans: 0.0035
Q5. Maximum strain in steel for Fe415 at failure is: Ans: 0.0038 (0.002 + 0.87fy/Es)
Q6. Characteristic strength fck means: Ans: Strength below which not more than 5% of test results are expected to fall
Q7. Load factor for DL + LL combination in limit state is: Ans: 1.5DL + 1.5LL
Q8. Load factor for DL + LL + WL combination is: Ans: 1.2DL + 1.2LL + 1.2WL
Q9. Modular ratio for M20 concrete is: Ans: m = 280/3σcbc = 280/(3×7) = 13.33
Q10. In working stress method, the stress-strain relationship is assumed: Ans: Linear (elastic)

1.2 Beams - Singly & Doubly Reinforced

Q11. Balanced section in limit state design for Fe415: xu/d = : Ans: 0.48
Q12. Balanced section for Fe500: xu,max/d = : Ans: 0.46
Q13. Balanced section for Fe250: xu,max/d = : Ans: 0.53
Q14. Minimum tension reinforcement in beams as per IS 456: Ans: As,min = 0.85bd/fy
Q15. Maximum reinforcement in a beam is: Ans: 4% of gross cross-sectional area (0.04bD)
Q16. Minimum shear reinforcement spacing as per IS 456: Ans: Sv = 0.87fy×Asv/(0.4b)
Q17. Maximum spacing of shear reinforcement: Ans: 0.75d or 300mm, whichever is less
Q18. Moment of resistance of balanced section (Fe415, M20): Mu,lim = : Ans: 0.138fck×b×d²
Q19. Moment of resistance for Fe500: Mu,lim = : Ans: 0.133fck×b×d²
Q20. A doubly reinforced beam is designed when: Ans: Mu > Mu,lim (moment exceeds balanced section capacity)
Q21. Effective depth of beam is measured from: Ans: Compression face to centroid of tension reinforcement
Q22. Clear cover for beams as per IS 456: Ans: 25mm (or diameter of bar, whichever is greater)
Q23. Nominal cover for column: Ans: 40mm
Q24. Nominal cover for footing: Ans: 50mm
Q25. Side face reinforcement is provided when depth exceeds: Ans: 750mm (0.1% of web area on each face)

1.3 Slabs

Q26. A slab is one-way when Ly/Lx ratio is: Ans: > 2
Q27. A slab is two-way when Ly/Lx ratio is: Ans: ≤ 2
Q28. Minimum thickness of slab as per IS 456: Ans: 75mm (general), 100mm (with mild exposure)
Q29. Distribution steel in one-way slab: Ans: 0.12% for HYSD bars, 0.15% for mild steel
Q30. Span/depth ratio for simply supported one-way slab: Ans: 20 (for basic value, modified by factors)
Q31. Span/depth ratio for cantilever slab: Ans: 7
Q32. Span/depth ratio for continuous slab: Ans: 26
Q33. Maximum spacing of main reinforcement in slab: Ans: 3d or 300mm, whichever is less
Q34. Maximum spacing of distribution steel in slab: Ans: 5d or 450mm, whichever is less
Q35. In two-way slab, shorter span carries: Ans: More load (higher BM coefficient)

1.4 Columns

Q36. A column is short when Le/D ≤ : Ans: 12 (or Le/b ≤ 12)
Q37. Minimum eccentricity in column as per IS 456: Ans: emin = L/500 + D/30, not less than 20mm
Q38. Axial load capacity of short column (IS 456): Ans: Pu = 0.4fck×Ac + 0.67fy×Asc
Q39. Minimum reinforcement in column: Ans: 0.8% of gross area
Q40. Maximum reinforcement in column: Ans: 6% of gross area (4% preferred for practical)
Q41. Minimum number of bars in rectangular column: Ans: 4
Q42. Minimum number of bars in circular column: Ans: 6
Q43. Lateral ties spacing in column: Ans: Least of: (i) least lateral dimension, (ii) 16×dia of longitudinal bar, (iii) 300mm
Q44. Minimum diameter of lateral ties: Ans: ¼ of largest longitudinal bar diameter or 6mm, whichever is more
Q45. Effective length of column with both ends fixed: Ans: 0.65L (as per IS 456 Table 28)

1.5 Footings & Development Length

Q46. Development length formula: Ld = : Ans: Ld = φ×σs/(4×τbd) = 0.87fy×φ/(4×τbd)
Q47. Bond stress τbd for M20 with Fe415: Ans: 1.2 N/mm² (increased by 60% for deformed bars = 1.92)
Q48. Development length for Fe415 in M20 concrete: Ans: Ld = 47φ (approximately)
Q49. Punching shear in footing is checked at: Ans: d/2 from face of column
Q50. One-way shear in footing is checked at: Ans: d from face of column
Q51. BM in isolated footing is calculated at: Ans: Face of column
Q52. Minimum depth of footing at edge: Ans: 150mm
Q53. Lap length for compression bars: Ans: Ld or 24φ, whichever is greater
Q54. Lap length for tension bars: Ans: Ld or 30φ, whichever is greater (for contact laps)
Q55. Anchorage value of a standard 90° bend: Ans: 8φ

1.6 Prestressed Concrete

Q56. In pre-tensioning, tendons are stressed: Ans: Before casting of concrete
Q57. In post-tensioning, tendons are stressed: Ans: After concrete has hardened
Q58. Losses in prestress include: Ans: Elastic shortening, creep, shrinkage, relaxation, friction, anchorage slip
Q59. Kern distance for rectangular section: Ans: D/6 from centroid
Q60. Concordant cable profile produces: Ans: No secondary moments (thrust line coincides with pressure line)
Q61. Minimum grade of concrete for prestressed concrete: Ans: M30 (pre-tensioned), M30 (post-tensioned)
Q62. Freyssinet system uses: Ans: High-tensile steel wires with conical anchorage
Q63. Magnel-Blaton system uses: Ans: Flat cables with sandwich plate anchorage
Q64. Loss due to elastic shortening in pre-tensioning: Ans: (m × fc) where fc = stress in concrete at steel level
Q65. Wobble coefficient in post-tensioning accounts for: Ans: Unintentional variation of duct from specified profile

2. Fluid Mechanics & Hydraulics (15-18 Questions Expected)

🎯 Exam Tip: Focus on Bernoulli's equation applications, flow through pipes, open channel flow (Manning's, Chezy's), hydraulic jump, and dimensional analysis. 3-4 numericals expected.

2.1 Fluid Properties & Pressure

Q66. Specific gravity of mercury is: Ans: 13.6
Q67. Kinematic viscosity = : Ans: μ/ρ (dynamic viscosity / density), unit: m²/s or Stoke
Q68. Pascal's law states: Ans: Pressure at a point in a static fluid is equal in all directions
Q69. Hydrostatic pressure on a vertical surface acts at: Ans: Centre of pressure (below centroid by Ig/Aȳ)
Q70. Centre of pressure for a vertical rectangular surface from free surface: Ans: 2h/3 from surface (h = depth of plate)
Q71. Buoyant force equals: Ans: Weight of fluid displaced (Archimedes' principle)
Q72. Metacentric height for stable floating body: Ans: GM = BM - BG = I/V - BG (must be positive)
Q73. A floating body is stable when: Ans: Metacentre is above centre of gravity (GM > 0)
Q74. Pressure at a depth h in a fluid: Ans: P = ρgh (gauge) or P = Patm + ρgh (absolute)
Q75. Manometer measures: Ans: Pressure difference between two points

2.2 Fluid Kinematics & Dynamics

Q76. Continuity equation for incompressible flow: Ans: A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
Q77. Bernoulli's equation: Ans: P/ρg + V²/2g + z = constant (along streamline)
Q78. Bernoulli's equation is based on: Ans: Conservation of energy for ideal fluid flow
Q79. Venturimeter measures: Ans: Discharge (flow rate) in a pipe
Q80. Coefficient of discharge for venturimeter: Ans: 0.95 to 0.98
Q81. Pitot tube measures: Ans: Velocity of flow at a point
Q82. Velocity from pitot tube: V = : Ans: Cv × √(2gh) where h = stagnation head
Q83. Reynolds number = : Ans: Re = ρVD/μ = VD/ν (inertia force / viscous force)
Q84. Flow is laminar when Re < : Ans: 2000 (for pipe flow)
Q85. Flow is turbulent when Re > : Ans: 4000 (for pipe flow)
Q86. Froude number = : Ans: Fr = V/√(gL) (inertia force / gravity force)
Q87. Mach number = : Ans: V/C (velocity / speed of sound)
Q88. Euler number relates: Ans: Pressure force to inertia force
Q89. Weber number relates: Ans: Inertia force to surface tension force
Q90. Momentum equation is based on: Ans: Newton's second law of motion

2.3 Flow Through Pipes

Q91. Darcy-Weisbach equation: hf = : Ans: fLV²/2gD (f = friction factor, L = length, D = diameter)
Q92. For laminar flow in pipe, friction factor f = : Ans: 64/Re
Q93. Velocity distribution in laminar pipe flow is: Ans: Parabolic
Q94. Ratio of maximum to average velocity in laminar pipe flow: Ans: 2 (Vmax = 2Vavg)
Q95. Hagen-Poiseuille equation gives: Ans: Q = πd⁴ΔP/(128μL) for laminar flow
Q96. Minor losses in pipes include: Ans: Entry, exit, sudden expansion, sudden contraction, bends, valves
Q97. Head loss due to sudden expansion: Ans: hL = (V₁-V₂)²/2g
Q98. Hydraulic gradient line (HGL) represents: Ans: P/ρg + z (pressure head + elevation head)
Q99. Total energy line (TEL) represents: Ans: P/ρg + V²/2g + z (always above HGL by V²/2g)
Q100. Water hammer in pipes is caused by: Ans: Sudden closure of valve (pressure wave)
Q101. Equivalent pipe replaces a compound pipe system with: Ans: Same head loss and same discharge
Q102. Hardy-Cross method is used for: Ans: Analysis of pipe networks
Q103. Moody diagram relates: Ans: Friction factor vs Reynolds number for different relative roughness

2.4 Open Channel Flow

Q104. Manning's equation: V = : Ans: (1/n) × R²/³ × S¹/² (n = roughness, R = hydraulic radius, S = slope)
Q105. Chezy's equation: V = : Ans: C√(RS) (C = Chezy's constant)
Q106. Hydraulic radius R = : Ans: A/P (area / wetted perimeter)
Q107. Most economical rectangular channel has: Ans: Width = 2 × depth (b = 2d)
Q108. Most economical trapezoidal channel: Ans: Half-hexagon shape (R = d/2)
Q109. Critical depth in rectangular channel: yc = : Ans: (q²/g)^(1/3) where q = Q/b
Q110. Froude number for critical flow: Ans: Fr = 1
Q111. Subcritical flow: Fr < 1, depth is: Ans: Greater than critical depth (tranquil flow)
Q112. Supercritical flow: Fr > 1, depth is: Ans: Less than critical depth (rapid/shooting flow)
Q113. Specific energy E = : Ans: y + V²/2g = y + Q²/(2gA²)
Q114. Hydraulic jump occurs when flow changes from: Ans: Supercritical to subcritical (energy dissipation)
Q115. Sequent depth ratio for hydraulic jump: y₂/y₁ = : Ans: ½(√(1+8Fr₁²) - 1)
Q116. Energy loss in hydraulic jump: Ans: ΔE = (y₂-y₁)³/(4y₁y₂)
Q117. Gradually varied flow is classified by: Ans: Channel slope (mild, steep, critical, horizontal, adverse) and zone (1,2,3)
Q118. M1 profile occurs: Ans: Upstream of a dam on mild slope (backwater curve)
Q119. S2 profile occurs: Ans: Downstream of a sluice gate on steep slope
Q120. Notch/Weir discharge formula (rectangular): Q = : Ans: (2/3)Cd×b×√(2g)×H^(3/2)

3. Soil Mechanics & Foundation Engineering (15-18 Questions Expected)

🎯 Exam Tip: Focus on index properties, permeability, consolidation, shear strength, bearing capacity (Terzaghi), earth pressure, and SPT correlations. Very scoring subject!

3.1 Index Properties & Classification

Q121. Void ratio e = : Ans: Vv/Vs (volume of voids / volume of solids)
Q122. Porosity n = : Ans: Vv/V = e/(1+e)
Q123. Degree of saturation S = : Ans: Vw/Vv (volume of water / volume of voids)
Q124. Relationship: Se = wGs: Ans: True (S=saturation, e=void ratio, w=water content, Gs=specific gravity)
Q125. Dry density γd = : Ans: γ/(1+w) = Gs×γw/(1+e)
Q126. Saturated density γsat = : Ans: (Gs+e)γw/(1+e)
Q127. Submerged density γ' = : Ans: γsat - γw = (Gs-1)γw/(1+e)
Q128. Liquid limit is determined by: Ans: Casagrande apparatus (25 blows) or cone penetrometer (20mm penetration)
Q129. Plasticity index PI = : Ans: LL - PL (liquid limit - plastic limit)
Q130. Activity of clay = : Ans: PI / (% clay fraction < 2μ)
Q131. IS soil classification: CL means: Ans: Clay of low plasticity (LL < 35%)
Q132. IS soil classification: CH means: Ans: Clay of high plasticity (LL > 50%)
Q133. A-line equation in plasticity chart: Ans: PI = 0.73(LL - 20)
Q134. Soils above A-line are: Ans: Clays (C)
Q135. Soils below A-line are: Ans: Silts (M) or organic soils (O)

3.2 Permeability & Seepage

Q136. Darcy's law: v = : Ans: ki (k = permeability, i = hydraulic gradient)
Q137. Constant head permeability test is used for: Ans: Coarse-grained soils (sand, gravel)
Q138. Falling head permeability test is used for: Ans: Fine-grained soils (silt, clay)
Q139. Equivalent permeability for flow parallel to layers: Ans: kH = (k₁H₁ + k₂H₂)/(H₁+H₂) (weighted average)
Q140. Equivalent permeability for flow perpendicular to layers: Ans: H/kV = H₁/k₁ + H₂/k₂ (harmonic mean)
Q141. Quick sand condition occurs when: Ans: Upward seepage pressure = submerged weight (i_critical = (Gs-1)/(1+e))
Q142. Critical hydraulic gradient for quick sand: Ans: ic ≈ 1.0 (for most soils)
Q143. Flow net consists of: Ans: Equipotential lines and flow lines (orthogonal)
Q144. Seepage discharge from flow net: Q = : Ans: k×H×(Nf/Nd) where Nf=flow channels, Nd=potential drops
Q145. Piping failure occurs due to: Ans: Backward erosion caused by seepage at exit point

3.3 Compaction & Consolidation

Q146. Standard Proctor test: rammer weight and drop: Ans: 2.6 kg, 310mm drop, 3 layers, 25 blows
Q147. Modified Proctor test: rammer weight and drop: Ans: 4.89 kg, 450mm drop, 5 layers, 25 blows
Q148. Zero air void line represents: Ans: 100% saturation (theoretical maximum dry density)
Q149. Terzaghi's consolidation theory assumes: Ans: Soil is homogeneous, saturated, flow is 1D, Darcy's law valid
Q150. Coefficient of consolidation Cv = : Ans: k/(mv×γw) where mv = coefficient of volume compressibility
Q151. Time factor Tv = : Ans: Cv×t/d² (d = drainage path)
Q152. For 50% consolidation, Tv = : Ans: 0.197
Q153. For 90% consolidation, Tv = : Ans: 0.848
Q154. Compression index Cc for normally consolidated clay: Ans: Cc = 0.009(LL-10) (Terzaghi & Peck)
Q155. Over-consolidation ratio OCR = : Ans: σ'p/σ'0 (preconsolidation pressure / present effective stress)

3.4 Shear Strength & Bearing Capacity

Q156. Mohr-Coulomb failure criterion: τf = : Ans: c + σ'tanφ (cohesion + friction)
Q157. UU test gives: Ans: φu = 0, cu = undrained cohesion (for saturated clay)
Q158. Drained test gives: Ans: Effective stress parameters c' and φ'
Q159. Vane shear test is used for: Ans: In-situ undrained shear strength of soft clays
Q160. Terzaghi's bearing capacity equation (strip footing): qu = : Ans: cNc + qNq + 0.5γBNγ
Q161. For square footing, Terzaghi's equation: qu = : Ans: 1.3cNc + qNq + 0.4γBNγ
Q162. For circular footing: qu = : Ans: 1.3cNc + qNq + 0.3γBNγ
Q163. Factor of safety for bearing capacity: Ans: FOS = qult/qnet (usually 2.5 to 3)
Q164. SPT N-value correction for overburden: Ans: N₁ = CN × N (CN = √(100/σ'v) for σ'v in kPa)
Q165. Plate load test gives: Ans: Bearing capacity and settlement of soil

3.5 Earth Pressure & Retaining Walls

Q166. Rankine's active earth pressure coefficient Ka = : Ans: (1-sinφ)/(1+sinφ) = tan²(45-φ/2)
Q167. Rankine's passive earth pressure coefficient Kp = : Ans: (1+sinφ)/(1-sinφ) = tan²(45+φ/2)
Q168. At rest earth pressure coefficient K₀ = : Ans: 1 - sinφ (Jaky's formula for NC clay)
Q169. Active pressure acts when wall moves: Ans: Away from soil (wall moves outward)
Q170. Passive pressure acts when wall moves: Ans: Towards soil (wall pushed into soil)
Q171. Coulomb's theory considers: Ans: Wall friction, inclined backfill, and battered wall face
Q172. For stability of retaining wall, FOS against overturning ≥ : Ans: 2.0
Q173. FOS against sliding for retaining wall ≥ : Ans: 1.5
Q174. Resultant should lie within middle third to avoid: Ans: Tension at base
Q175. Depth of tension crack in cohesive soil: zc = : Ans: 2c/(γ√Ka)
